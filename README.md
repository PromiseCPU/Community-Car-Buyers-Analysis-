#  COMMUNITY CAR BUYERS ANALYSIS.
## Overview:
A data analysis project examining how financial and demographic factors influence car purchasing behavior within a community using Microsoft Excel and basic statistical techniques.

## Sector:
Finance, Transport

## Problem Statement:
Assess how financial and demographic factors influence car purchasing behavior.

## Tools Used:
MS Excel, Basic Statistics

## Dataset
Excel-based sample dataset sourced from YouTube. (Show dataset)
(https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/15v2P0m6s-w1TxFSyKayEUSJKzQ9H8QYp/edit)

## Questions/ Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):
. Age groups with the highest car purchases
. Gender distribution of car buyers
. Commute distance associated with the highest purchases

## Process:
. Data sourcing and validation
. Data cleaning (duplicates, outliers, and standardization)
. Age binning for improved analysis
. Pivot table creation and visualization
. Dashboard development and reporting

## Visualization: (snipping tool)
Excel dashboard created using pivot charts.
(https://docs.google.com/document/d/19dvbk05U3KT58r0mQZCMp74kJDnnbiOpMKtGvO7WlvQ/edit)

## Key Insights:
. Males purchased more cars and had higher average income levels.
. Individuals aged 31–55 recorded the highest car purchases.
. Commute distances of 0–1 miles showed the highest ownership rates.

## Conclusion 
This project analyzed the impact of financial and demographic factors on car purchasing behavior using Excel and statistical tools. The results showed that middle-aged individuals (31–55) and males purchased more cars, largely due to higher income levels. Short commute distances (0–1 miles) recorded the highest number of car purchases, highlighting lifestyle and convenience factors.

## Recommendation:
Financial institutions should design targeted car financing options for middle-aged earners, who represent the largest buyer segment. Auto dealers can expand market reach by developing inclusive marketing and flexible payment plans, especially for underrepresented groups. Urban planners and policymakers should consider these commuting and ownership patterns when shaping transport and mobility policies.
